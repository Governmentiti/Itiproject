Computer Operator and Programming Assistant (COPA)

Overview

The COPA trade is one of the most popular courses in ITI that introduces students to the world of computers, software, and basic programming. It is designed to develop fundamental IT skills necessary in almost every industry today. Whether you aspire to become a data entry operator, office assistant, or software tester, COPA provides the essential foundation.

What You Will Learn

The course includes computer basics, operating systems (Windows, Linux), MS Office, Internet and Email, basic programming concepts (HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Python), database management, and basic hardware knowledge. You'll also learn about cybersecurity, computer networking, and hands-on work in software installations and troubleshooting.

Career Opportunities

After completing the COPA trade, students can get jobs in private companies, schools, hospitals, shops, and government offices as computer operators, data entry operators, help desk assistants, or clerical support. It also opens opportunities in call centers, software firms, and back-office operations. Freelancing and working online as a virtual assistant or data entry specialist are also good options.

Course Duration and Eligibility

COPA is a 1-year course (2 semesters). Students who have passed the 10th standard are eligible. No prior computer knowledge is required. The course is easy to follow, and the practical sessions make learning interactive and job-focused.

Higher Education and Career Growth

After COPA, students can pursue diplomas in computer applications, web design, or software engineering. Many opt for polytechnic or BCA courses. With experience, you can grow into roles such as IT support executive, system administrator, or junior programmer. It also serves as a stepping stone to competitive exams where computer knowledge is essential.
