Mechanic Diesel Trade

Overview

The Mechanic Diesel trade is a specialized technical program focused on the maintenance and repair of diesel engines and related systems. This trade is designed for students who are interested in automobiles, heavy vehicles, and power machines. From basic engine components to full system diagnostics, this trade teaches students to work with real-world diesel machinery.

What You Will Learn

During the course, students are trained in dismantling and assembling diesel engines, servicing fuel injection systems, lubricating systems, cooling systems, and electrical circuits. They also learn fault finding, engine overhauling, and emission control systems. Practical sessions include working with tractors, trucks, buses, generators, and other diesel-powered machines.

Career Opportunities

After completing this trade, students can find employment in automobile workshops, transport corporations, agricultural machinery services, mining companies, construction companies, and public sector units. They can work as diesel mechanics, service engineers, supervisors, or maintenance personnel. Skilled diesel mechanics are in demand in both government and private sectors.

Course Duration and Eligibility

The Mechanic Diesel trade is a 1-year course (2 semesters). Candidates must have passed at least 10th standard to be eligible. The course offers a strong foundation in mechanical skills and prepares students for real-world job scenarios quickly and effectively.

Higher Education and Career Growth

After completing the trade, students can go for diploma courses in automobile or mechanical engineering. They can also pursue skill development programs offered by NSDC and other government schemes. With experience, one can move into supervisory roles or open their own automobile service center. Diesel mechanics also have good opportunities abroad, especially in the Middle East and Europe.
