Fitter Trade

Overview

The Fitter trade in ITI is a mechanical trade that focuses on the assembly, fitting, and maintenance of different types of machinery and mechanical systems. It prepares students for a wide range of tasks in industrial settings, where mechanical fitting, adjustments, and inspections are routine. This course is ideal for those who enjoy working with tools, machines, and have a mechanical aptitude.

What You Will Learn

Students in the Fitter trade gain knowledge in fitting, assembling parts, filing, grinding, drilling, maintenance of machines, and operating mechanical tools. They learn about different types of joints, tolerance levels, measuring instruments like vernier calipers and micrometers, and the importance of precision in engineering. Safety practices and reading mechanical blueprints are also important parts of the syllabus.

Career Opportunities

Fitter trade graduates are in demand in various industries such as manufacturing, steel plants, railways, shipyards, and private workshops. They can work as maintenance fitters, machine operators, fabrication fitters, and assembly technicians. With experience, they can grow into supervisory roles or become self-employed by opening their own workshops. The mechanical field offers stable and skill-based employment across sectors.

Course Duration and Eligibility

The Fitter trade course is 2 years in duration (4 semesters). To be eligible for this course, candidates must have completed their 10th grade (High School) from a recognized board. A basic understanding of mathematics and interest in tools and machinery will help students perform better in this trade.

Higher Education and Advancement

After completing the ITI Fitter trade, students can pursue diploma or advanced vocational courses in mechanical engineering. They can also take competitive exams for government jobs in PSUs, railways, and defense. Those aiming for entrepreneurship can open fabrication units or mechanical service workshops. With further experience, they can also work abroad in industries that require mechanical fitters.
