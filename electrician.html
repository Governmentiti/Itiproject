Electrician Trade

Overview

The Electrician trade in ITI is one of the most sought-after technical courses, designed to impart theoretical and practical knowledge of electrical systems and devices. The course trains students in wiring, maintenance, repair of household and industrial electrical systems, safety procedures, and the operation of different electrical machinery. This course is crucial for the development of skilled professionals needed in the infrastructure and manufacturing sectors of the economy.

What You Will Learn

During the course, students gain hands-on experience in installing, maintaining, and repairing electrical wiring and equipment. Key topics include electrical circuits, transformers, motors, generators, switches, power distribution, and safety devices. The curriculum also covers fault diagnosis, preventive maintenance, and the use of testing instruments like multimeters and megohmmeters.

Career Opportunities

After completing the course, Electrician trade students have diverse career paths. They can work as domestic electricians, maintenance electricians in factories, or in large companies that manage infrastructure. They may also find roles in government electricity boards, railways, construction companies, or set up their own electrical repair businesses. The demand for skilled electricians is consistent due to the ever-increasing usage of electricity in every aspect of life.

Course Duration and Eligibility

The course duration for the Electrician trade is 2 years (4 semesters). The minimum eligibility criteria for enrolling in this trade is completion of 10th standard (High School) from a recognized board. Students with a basic understanding of science and math often find the course easier to grasp.

Higher Education and Advancement

After ITI, students can pursue advanced diploma courses in electrical engineering or enroll in polytechnic courses. With experience and further certifications, electricians can progress to roles like Electrical Supervisor, Foreman, or even start their own contracting business. They can also appear in government job exams such as SSC, RRB, or state-level technician recruitments.
