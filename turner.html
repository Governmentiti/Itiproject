Turner Trade

Overview

The Turner trade focuses on the operation of lathes and the precision shaping of metal parts. It includes the turning process where a piece of material is rotated and a cutting tool is applied to create shapes like cylindrical parts, shafts, and bushings. A Turner works with various machine tools to produce intricate components used in the manufacturing of machinery, vehicles, and electronics.

What You Will Learn

In the Turner trade, students gain expertise in operating various lathe machines such as plain, engine, and CNC lathes. They also learn about turning operations like drilling, facing, threading, and tapering. Other important skills include precision measurement techniques, blueprint reading, and tool setting. Students also learn about machine maintenance and safety practices to ensure smooth operations and reduce hazards.

Career Opportunities

After completing the Turner trade, students can work in industries such as automotive, aerospace, shipbuilding, and manufacturing. Job roles include Turner, Lathe Operator, CNC Programmer, and Production Technician. With enough experience, students can also move into supervisory roles or take on roles like tool setter, quality control manager, or machine shop manager.

Course Duration and Eligibility

The Turner course usually lasts for 2 years (4 semesters). Candidates who have completed Class 10 with Mathematics and Science are eligible for this course. It is best suited for those interested in hands-on work with machines and precision metalworking.

Higher Education and Growth

Turner students can opt for further studies in mechanical engineering, CAD design, or CNC machining. With further education, they can pursue higher-level positions such as Production Manager, Mechanical Engineer, or Design Engineer. Many Turners also opt for certification courses to specialize in advanced manufacturing technologies and automation.
